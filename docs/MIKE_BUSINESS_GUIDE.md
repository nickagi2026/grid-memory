## 1. What Is MIKE?

### What is MIKE?

MIKE is an **executive intelligence layer** that sits on top of your organization's memory and surfaces what matters: opportunities, risks, decisions, and their monetary impact.

Think of MIKE as the operating system for organizational intelligence. It ingests everything your teams and agents decide, observe, and produce — then continuously answers the only question that matters:

> *"What should we do next, why should we do it, how confident are we, what happens if we don't, and how much money is involved?"*

### What problem does MIKE solve?

Organizations have a **memory problem**. Decisions get made and forgotten. Risks get identified and lost. Opportunities get surfaced and never pursued. When people leave, their knowledge leaves with them. When teams grow, they repeat past mistakes.

This is not a technology problem. It's an **organizational amnesia** problem.

MIKE solves it by making organizational memory active — not a static archive, but a living intelligence layer that continuously surfaces what matters.

### Why was MIKE created?

Because the gap between having data and having actionable intelligence is where most organizations fail.

Every organization has:
- **Slack channels** where decisions are buried
- **Notion docs** that nobody reads
- **Jira tickets** that close without outcomes tracked
- **Meetings** where the same problems get re-debated every quarter
- **Spreadsheets** that are wrong the moment they're saved

MIKE was built to turn this noise into signal — automatically, continuously, and in business terms.

### Who is MIKE for?

| Role | What MIKE Does For Them |
|------|------------------------|
| **CEO** | Answers: "What should I focus on this week?" Shows opportunities, risks, revenue trajectory |
| **CTO** | Answers: "What are my teams actually deciding? Are we repeating mistakes?" Surfaces decision patterns |
| **Consulting Partner** | Answers: "What's happening across my clients? Where are the expansion opportunities?" Generates QBRs automatically |
| **PE Operator** | Answers: "What's the real risk in this portfolio company? Where's the hidden value?" Monitors organizational health |
| **Fractional Executive** | Answers: "I have 10 clients — what needs my attention right now?" Delivers an executive dashboard for each |
| **Head of AI** | Answers: "What are my agents doing? Are they aligned? Are they hallucinating?" Provides provenance and audit |

### How is MIKE different from ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a conversation. You ask, it answers, you forget.

MIKE is continuous. It watches everything, remembers everything, and surfaces what matters without being asked. ChatGPT has no memory of your business. MIKE is your business's memory.

ChatGPT answers questions. MIKE answers: *"What should I do next?"*

### How is MIKE different from Copilot?

Copilot is embedded in a tool (code, docs, meetings). It helps you complete tasks within that tool.

MIKE operates across all your tools. It doesn't help you write an email — it tells you which email you should be writing, and why it's worth $200K.

### How is MIKE different from BI tools?

BI tools (Tableau, Looker, PowerBI) answer questions you already know to ask. You build dashboards. You write queries. You interpret the results.

MIKE answers questions you didn't know to ask. It surfaces opportunities you didn't see. It alerts you to risks you didn't notice. BI is rear-view mirror. MIKE is the windshield.

### How is MIKE different from consulting firms?

Consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte) deliver a point-in-time report. You pay $500K, get a PDF, and six months later it's outdated.

MIKE is continuous. It doesn't deliver a report — it maintains a living intelligence layer that updates every time your team makes a decision. MIKE costs a fraction of a consulting engagement and never stops delivering.

### How is MIKE different from agent platforms?

Agent platforms (LangGraph, CrewAI, AutoGen) are frameworks for building agent workflows. They help agents communicate, but they don't help executives understand what those agents are doing or whether it's working.

MIKE sits on top of agent platforms and translates agent activity into business intelligence. It answers: "What did my 47 agents decide today, and how much is it worth?"

## 2. What Business Problems Does MIKE Solve?

### How does MIKE help increase revenue?

MIKE identifies opportunities that are hidden in your organization's daily operations:

- **Upsell opportunities** — A client mentions a new pain point. MIKE flags it as an expansion opportunity with estimated value.

- **Pipeline gaps** — MIKE tracks win/loss patterns and identifies which stages are leaking revenue.

### How does MIKE help reduce costs?

- **Duplicate work** — MIKE detects when two teams solve the same problem independently
- **Repeated mistakes** — MIKE tracks decision outcomes and flags when a decision pattern has historically failed
- **Onboarding time** — MIKE preserves institutional knowledge, cutting new hire ramp time from months to days

Every decision, rationale, outcome, and lesson is stored in tamper-evident, append-only memory. When someone leaves:

- Their decisions don't leave with them
- Their rationale is preserved
- Their lessons are available to successors

Due diligence is exponentially faster with MIKE:

- Full audit trail of every decision

### How does MIKE help during AI transformations?

As organizations deploy more AI agents, they create a new class of risk: agent decisions without human oversight. MIKE:

Consulting firms face a fundamental scaling problem: quality degrades as headcount grows because knowledge is in people's heads. MIKE:

- Preserves engagement knowledge across teams
- Enables junior consultants to operate at senior level with full context

## 4. How Does MIKE Work?

### How does MIKE collect information?

MIKE collects information through the Grid — a persistent memory layer that agents and humans write to:

- **Agent writes**: When subagents complete work, they write decisions, facts, and observations
- **API writes**: Integrations push data from Slack, Jira, Notion, email
- **Direct writes**: Humans enter decisions and observations directly via the API or CLI

### Where does MIKE get its data?

| Source | What MIKE Gets |
|--------|---------------|
| Agent decisions | Structured decisions with rationale |
| Subagent outputs | Completed tasks, blockers, observations |
| API integrations | Slack messages, Jira tickets, Notion docs |
| Direct user entry | Strategic decisions, meeting notes |
| Federation | Data from connected Grid instances |

### How does MIKE learn?

MIKE learns through **pattern recognition**:

1. **Observation**: Agents write decisions, facts, outcomes
2. **Analysis**: MIKE correlates decisions with outcomes
3. **Pattern detection**: MIKE identifies what produces good outcomes
4. **Recommendation**: MIKE surfaces patterns as recommendations
5. **Verification**: Humans confirm or reject recommendations

### How does MIKE remember?

MIKE uses **append-only memory** with tiered retention:

- **Working memory** (hours): Active context, high churn
- **Project memory** (weeks): Confirmed decisions, validated outcomes
- **Organizational memory** (years): Curated knowledge, permanent

Nothing is overwritten. Everything is timestamped. Every entry has a traceable provenance chain.

### How does MIKE identify patterns?

MIKE identifies patterns by analyzing:

- **Tag correlations**: Which tags co-occur with successful outcomes
- **Temporal patterns**: How decisions evolve over time
- **Agent patterns**: Which agents produce reliable vs unreliable information
- **Decision-outcome mappings**: Which decision patterns lead to which outcomes
- **Contradiction detection**: Where information conflicts

### How does MIKE identify opportunities?

MIKE's opportunity engine continuously scans for:

- **Revenue signals**: Content mentioning value, budget, investment
- **Pain signals**: Content mentioning problems, blockers, issues
- **Gap signals**: Topics discussed but never acted upon
- **Pattern signals**: Solutions working in one area that could apply elsewhere

Each opportunity is scored on:
- **Estimated value**: Dollar amount based on similar past opportunities
- **Confidence**: How sure MIKE is this is a real opportunity
- **Effort**: Estimated time/resources required
- **ROI**: Projected return on investment

### How does MIKE identify risks?

MIKE detects five categories of risk:

1. **Amnesia risk**: Topics not discussed in 30+ days
2. **Orphan risk**: Decisions made but never acted upon  
3. **Concentration risk**: Knowledge held by single individuals
4. **Contradiction risk**: Conflicting decisions across the organization
5. **Stale decision risk**: Decisions older than 60 days without review

### How does MIKE generate executive insights?

MIKE consolidates everything into an executive dashboard that answers:

- **Opportunities**: What should we pursue? (ranked by value × confidence)
- **Risks**: What should we worry about? (ranked by severity × probability)
- **Decisions**: What did we decide? (with rationale and outcomes)
- **Revenue**: How are we tracking? (pipeline, wins, losses, accuracy)
- **Health**: How is the organization doing? (amnesia score, SPOF count)

### How does MIKE avoid hallucinations?

MIKE doesn't generate content from a model — it **derives insights from stored data**:

- Every recommendation is grounded in actual entries
- Every opportunity is backed by supporting evidence
- Confidence scores communicate certainty level
- All insights are traceable to source entries

### How does MIKE validate recommendations?

Recommendations are validated through:

1. **Evidence count**: How many entries support this pattern
2. **Outcome correlation**: What percentage of similar patterns produced good outcomes
3. **Temporal recency**: How recent is the supporting data
4. **Agent reputation**: How reliable are the agents that provided the data

## 5. Opportunity Engine

> This is one of MIKE's most valuable capabilities. The Opportunity Engine turns passive data into active revenue.

### What is an opportunity?

An opportunity is a **revenue-generating action** that MIKE identifies from your organization's daily operations. Opportunities range from upsells to new service lines to strategic pivots.

### How does MIKE identify opportunities?

MIKE continuously scans for four types of signals:

| Signal Type | What MIKE Looks For | Example |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| **Pain** | Words like "problem", "issue", "blocking", "urgent", "frustrated" | Client mentions migration is too slow |
| **Gap** | Topics discussed but never resolved or acted upon | "We should explore AI for customer support" — said 3 times, never done |
| **Pattern** | Solutions working in one area that could apply elsewhere | DevOps automation that worked for team A, not yet applied to team B |
| **Competitive** | Competitor names and market movements | "Acme just launched feature X" — flagged for competitive response |

### How does MIKE score opportunities?

Each opportunity gets a priority score based on:

```
priority = estimated_value × (confidence × stage_weight) / effort
```

| Factor | Weight | How It's Determined |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Estimated value | 0–$1M+ | Based on similar past opportunities and revenue outcomes |
| Confidence | 0–100% | Based on signal strength, evidence count, and pattern match |
| Stage weight | 0.3–0.9 | detected=0.3, reviewed=0.5, accepted=0.7, proposed=0.9 |
| Effort | hours | Estimated from similar projects in organizational memory |

### What evidence supports an opportunity?

Every opportunity is backed by the actual entries that triggered it:

- The Slack message where the client mentioned the problem
- The decision where the team explored the solution
- The outcome showing the financial impact
- The market research supporting the approach

### How does MIKE estimate value?

MIKE estimates value by:

1. Finding similar past opportunities in organizational memory
2. Extracting their actual revenue outcomes
3. Adjusting for context differences (client size, scope, geography)
4. Applying a confidence multiplier based on evidence quality

### How does MIKE estimate effort?

MIKE estimates effort by:

1. Finding similar completed projects in memory
2. Extracting actual hours and resources used
3. Adjusting for team composition and experience
4. Surfacing the range (best case / expected / worst case)

### How does MIKE estimate ROI?

```
ROI = (estimated_value × confidence) / (effort_cost × risk_factor)
```

MIKE surfaces both the raw ROI number and the confidence range, so you know not just the answer but how reliable the answer is.

### How does MIKE estimate confidence?

Confidence is a composite score:

| Factor | Weight | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Evidence count | 30% | How many entries support this opportunity |
| Pattern strength | 25% | How closely this matches past successful patterns |
| Temporal recency | 20% | How recent the supporting data is |
| Agent reputation | 15% | Reliability of the agents that surfaced signals |
| Outcome correlation | 10% | Historical accuracy of similar opportunities |

### How do I approve opportunities?

```bash
curl -X POST /auto-contracts/approve \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"scope": "upsell:client-acme", "suggested_schema": { ... }}'
```

Approved opportunities automatically:
1. Register as active projects
2. Appear on the executive dashboard
3. Link to supporting evidence
4. Track progress toward outcome

### How do I reject opportunities?

```bash
curl -X POST /auto-contracts/reject \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"scope": "opportunity:low-value-signal"}'
```

Rejected opportunities:
1. Won't be suggested again
2. Are logged for pattern analysis
3. Help MIKE learn what's not valuable

### How do I convert an opportunity into a project?

When you decide to pursue an opportunity:

1. Approve it (see above)
2. MIKE auto-generates a project structure
3. MIKE links the project to the opportunity's supporting evidence
4. MIKE tracks the outcome and compares it to the estimate (accuracy tracking)

This creates a closed loop: **signal → opportunity → project → outcome → learning → better signal detection.**

## 8. Decision Intelligence

> This is one of MIKE's biggest differentiators. Most organizations have no idea which of their decisions are good or bad. MIKE tracks every decision, measures every outcome, and learns from every result.

### What is a decision?

A decision is a **structured choice** with:
- **Who** made it
- **What** they decided
- **Why** (rationale)
- **When**
- **Alternatives considered**
- **Expected outcome**

### How does MIKE track decisions?

Every call to `POST /write` with `type: "decision"` is tracked as a decision. MIKE builds a **Decision Graph** — a map of every decision, its rationale, and its relationship to other decisions.

```bash
curl -X POST /write \
  -d '{"agent_id": "cto", "type": "decision",
       "content": "Migrate to PostgreSQL. Rationale: ACID compliance, team expertise.",
       "tags": ["topic:database", "project:infra"]}'
```

### How does MIKE track decision outcomes?

Decisions can have outcomes attached via `PATCH /entries/:id/outcome`:

```bash
curl -X PATCH /entries/grid_123/outcome \
  -d '{"outcome": "success", "delta": "40% latency reduction"}'
```

### How does MIKE identify good decisions?

MIKE identifies good decisions by:

1. Tracking outcome correlations
2. Analyzing which decision-makers have the highest success rates
3. Identifying which decision patterns produce consistent results
4. Surfacing the factors that correlate with success (e.g., decisions with written rationale succeed 73% more often)

### How does MIKE identify bad decisions?

MIKE identifies bad decisions by:

1. Tracking decisions that lack outcomes (orphans)
2. Analyzing decisions with negative outcomes
3. Detecting decisions that were later reversed
4. Surfacing decisions that contradicted successful patterns

### How does MIKE identify recurring decision patterns?

MIKE analyzes decisions by:

- **Topic clustering**: Which topics are being decided on
- **Agent grouping**: Who's making decisions on which topics
- **Temporal patterns**: How decisions evolve over time
- **Outcome correlation**: What patterns produce what results

### How does MIKE help prevent repeated mistakes?

MIKE's **amnesia detector** surfaces:

- **Orphaned decisions**: Made but never tracked to outcome
- **Contradictory decisions**: Two decisions pointing in opposite directions
- **Stale decisions**: Older than 60 days without review
- **Single points of failure**: Critical knowledge held by one person

### How does MIKE build organizational memory?

MIKE doesn't just store decisions — it builds a living graph:

```
Decision A (May 1)
  → Outcome: Success (May 15)
  → Lesson: "Always include rationale"
  → Applied to: Decision B (June 1)
    → Outcome: Success (June 10)
    → Pattern: "Rationale-driven decisions succeed 73% more often"
```

This graph becomes your organization's **institutional memory** — accessible to anyone, at any time, regardless of who's still at the company.

## 9. QBR Intelligence

### How does MIKE generate QBRs?

```bash
curl http://localhost:8080/qbr?period=Q1-2026
```

MIKE generates QBRs by:

1. Analyzing all entries from the period
2. Identifying wins, risks, decisions, and outcomes
3. Extracting KPIs and trends
4. Generating insights and recommendations
5. Producing a structured report

### What information is included?

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Executive Summary** | One-page overview of the quarter |

MIKE identifies wins by:

### How does MIKE identify risks?

MIKE identifies risks by scanning for:

- **Single points of failure**: Knowledge held by one person
- **Contradictions**: Conflicting decisions

### How does MIKE identify missed opportunities?

MIKE identifies missed opportunities by:

### How does MIKE identify next-quarter priorities?

MIKE surfaces:

- **Risk trends** that need monitoring

### How does MIKE identify strategic recommendations?

## 11. Risk Intelligence

> MIKE turns passive data into active risk detection. Most organizations discover risks when they become crises. MIKE surfaces them weeks or months earlier.

### How does MIKE identify risks?

MIKE continuously scans for five categories of organizational risk:

### What types of risks can MIKE detect?

| Risk Type | What MIKE Monitors | Alert Trigger |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Amnesia** | Topics not discussed recently | 30+ days since last mention |
| **Orphan** | Decisions without outcomes | 14+ days since decision made |
| **Stale** | Decisions without review | 60+ days since last update |
| **SPOF** | Single-person knowledge | Topic known by only one agent |
| **Contradiction** | Conflicting information | Two agents saying opposite things |

### How are risks scored?

```
risk_score = severity × probability × imminence
```

| Factor | Range | How It's Determined |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| **Severity** | 0–10 | Business impact if risk materializes |
| **Probability** | 0–1 | Likelihood based on pattern analysis |
| **Imminence** | 0–1 | How soon it could materialize |

### How are risks prioritized?

Risks are sorted by risk_score and grouped into:

- **Critical** (score 7+): Immediate attention required
- **High** (score 5–7): Action needed this week
- **Medium** (score 3–5): Monitor and plan
- **Low** (score <3): Track but no action needed

### How are risks escalated?

Critical risks appear on the executive dashboard with:
- The specific risk
- Supporting evidence
- Recommended mitigation
- Owner assignment

### How does MIKE detect project failure patterns?

MIKE detects patterns that historically preceded failures:

- **Silence**: No updates for extended periods (hidden problems)
- **Blocker accumulation**: Problems not being resolved
- **Scope creep**: Uncontrolled expansion of scope
- **Decision reversal**: Key decisions being revisited
- **Resource depletion**: Team members being pulled off

### How does MIKE detect organizational amnesia?

MIKE's amnesia detector runs continuously:

```bash
curl http://localhost:8080/amnesia/detect
```

It returns:

```json
{
  "gaps": [
    {"topic": "database migration", "last_referenced": "2026-04-01", "days_since": 59, "severity": "high"}
  ],
  "orphans": [
    {"decision": "Use MongoDB", "agent": "arch-agent", "days_orphaned": 30}
  ],
  "stale_decisions": [...],
  "single_points_of_failure": [
    {"topic": "AWS architecture", "held_by": "alice", "risk": "If alice leaves, knowledge is lost"}
  ],
  "amnesia_score": 0.35,
  "summary": "3 knowledge gaps, 2 orphaned decisions, 5 stale decisions, 1 single point of failure"
}
```

### How does MIKE detect knowledge concentration risk?

MIKE maps every topic to the agents that have written about it. When a topic is only covered by one agent, it's flagged as a **single point of failure**:

```
Topic: AWS Infrastructure
  → Written by: alice (only)
  → Risk: If alice leaves, AWS knowledge is lost
  → Severity: High (critical infrastructure)
```

## 12. Organizational Intelligence

> This is potentially MIKE's biggest moat. MIKE builds a living map of what your organization knows — and what it's forgetting.

### What is organizational intelligence?

Organizational intelligence is the sum of everything your organization knows, decides, and learns — preserved, accessible, and active. Not stored in people's heads. Not buried in Slack archives. Active and surfaced.

### What is organizational memory?

Organizational memory is the permanent record of:
- **Decisions**: What was decided, by whom, why
- **Outcomes**: What happened as a result
- **Lessons**: What was learned
- **Patterns**: What works, what doesn't
- **Knowledge**: Facts, observations, expertise

### How does MIKE preserve knowledge?

MIKE preserves knowledge by:

1. **Ingesting everything**: Every agent write is saved permanently
2. **Structuring nothing**: Tagged, typed, timestamped
3. **Tiering intelligently**: Working → Project → Organization (auto-promotion)
4. **Never overwriting**: Append-only, every version preserved
5. **Purging automatically**: TTLs handle what's temporary

### How does MIKE detect knowledge loss?

MIKE's amnesia detector surfaces:

- **Gaps**: Topics last discussed 30+ days ago
- **Orphans**: Decisions with no outcome tracked
- **SPOFs**: Knowledge held by a single agent
- **Stale decisions**: Older than 60 days without review

### How does MIKE identify tribal knowledge?

Tribal knowledge is information that's known to individuals but not written down. MIKE identifies it by:

- Topics that are referenced across multiple entries but never formally documented
- Decisions that rely on unwritten context
- Processes that are executed but never captured

### How does MIKE identify single points of failure?

MIKE maps every topic to its contributing agents. When a topic is only covered by one agent:

```
topic → agents = ["alice"]
→ SPOF flagged
→ Recommendation: "Alice has the only knowledge of AWS infrastructure.
                    Consider documenting or cross-training."
```

### How does MIKE identify critical experts?

Critical experts are agents whose knowledge covers the most topics, especially high-severity topics. MIKE ranks them:

```
1. Alice → covers 47 topics, 12 critical, 8 single points of failure
2. Bob → covers 31 topics, 5 critical, 2 single points of failure
```

### How does MIKE identify knowledge gaps?

Knowledge gaps are topics that are:
1. Important to the organization
2. Not covered by any agent
3. Actively needed (based on recent decisions)

MIKE surfaces these as gaps with a severity rating.

### How does MIKE use AI?

MIKE uses AI in two modes:

1. **Analysis**: Pattern matching, opportunity detection, risk identification (deterministic, high-confidence)

MIKE supports any OpenAI-compatible API:

- Anthropic (Claude 3, via API proxy)
- Open-source models (via OpenAI-compatible endpoints)
- Custom models (via API proxy)

### How does MIKE protect data?

- **Encryption at rest**: All data is stored with encryption
- **Encryption in transit**: All API calls use HTTPS
- **Workspace isolation**: Client data is fully isolated
- **PII detection**: Sensitive data is detected and can be blocked or redacted

### How does MIKE avoid hallucinations?

MIKE's insights are **derived from stored data, not generated by a model**:

- Every opportunity is backed by actual entries
- Every risk is based on real data patterns
- Every recommendation is supported by evidence
- AI is only used for summarization and narrative generation

### How does MIKE explain recommendations?

Every recommendation includes:

1. **What**: The specific action
2. **Why**: Supporting evidence with links to source entries
3. **How sure**: Confidence score with explanation
4. **What if not**: Potential consequences of inaction

### How does MIKE provide auditability?

Every operation is logged to a **tamper-evident audit trail** with HMAC-SHA256 hash chaining:

```bash
curl http://localhost:8080/gateway/audit/verify
# Returns: { "valid": true } or { "valid": false, "brokenAtIndex": 3 }
```

### How does MIKE provide traceability?

Every entry has a **provenance chain**:

```
Entry X (agent: arch-agent, type: decision)
  → Informed by Entry Y (agent: research-agent, type: fact)
  → Referenced in Entry Z (agent: ops-agent, type: observation)
```

This creates a traceable path from any insight back to its raw data.
